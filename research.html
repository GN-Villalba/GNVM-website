Publications & Research

PhD Research

  • The Effects of Accreditation Policy on Higher Education Outcomes in Colombia. This project evaluates the impact of Colombia’s mandatory high-quality accreditation policy on student outcomes in teacher training programmes. Using administrative data (SNIES, ICFES) and quasi-experimental methods (event study, IV), it examines whether accreditation improves student learning and how effects vary by institution type.
  • The Impact of T Levels on Education Outcomes in the UK. This project studies how the rollout of T Levels influences post-16 educational choices, completion, and equity across gender, socioeconomic background, and regions in England. Combining Difference-in-Differences estimates with a structural choice model, it explores mechanisms such as stigma, availability, and policy counterfactuals.

Research in Progress

  • Sticky Floors and Gendered Labour Structures: Discrimination, Education, and Household Barriers in Paraguay.
    Draft for CAF–WELAC 2025.
    PDF This paper investigates the gender pay gap in Paraguay, highlighting structural barriers that keep women in low-wage positions. It examines education, household constraints, and labour segmentation to explain the persistence of “sticky floors.”
  • Information and Adjustment of Expectations (Peru).
    Field experiment.
    Co-authors: Muchin Bazan (Pontificia Universidad Catolica de Chile.), Diego Esteban Quispe (Pontificia Universidad Catolica Perú)
    A randomised field experiment that measures how providing information about previous cohorts’ performance affects students’ academic expectations and choices in a competitive summer program in Peru.
  • Innovation and Productivity in Paraguay (CDM).
    Co-author: Selva Olmedo (Universidad de Santiago Compostela)
    Application of the Crépon–Duguet–Mairesse model to Paraguayan firm-level data. The study explores how innovation activities, knowledge protection, and sectoral heterogeneity affect productivity in manufacturing and services.
