About Me

I am a PhD candidate at the University of Sussex, specializing in Applied Microeconomics, with a focus on education, labor economics, and development economics.

My research examines the effects of public policies on educational quality, labor market inequalities, and higher education outcomes. I am particularly interested in causal inference methods, including Difference-in-Differences (DiD) and Event Study designs with Instrumental Variables (IV).

I often explore themes of development and inequality in the context of Latin America, where education and labor market dynamics present unique challenges and opportunities.

Beyond research, I am passionate about policy evaluation, social mobility, and the intersection of education and labor market outcomes. This website shares research insights, reflections, and useful resources for those interested in education policy, labor markets, inequality, and applied econometrics.
