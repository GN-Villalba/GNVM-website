About Me

I am a PhD candidate at the University of Sussex, specializing in Applied Microeconomics, with a focus on the economics of education, labour markets, and development economics.

My research examines the effects of public policies on educational quality, labour market inequalities, and higher education outcomes. I am particularly interested in causal inference methods, including including the use of Instrumental Variables (IV), Difference-in-Differences (DiD), and Event Study designs..

I often explore themes of development and inequality in the context of Latin America, where education and labour market dynamics present unique challenges and opportunities.

Beyond my core research, I am also interested in gender, social mobility, and the dynamics of less traditional markets — from fashion and sports to cultural behaviors. I’m drawn to understanding how economic forces shape, and are shaped by, the choices people make in these unique spaces.

This website is a space where I share research insights, reflections, and resources — especially for those interested in education policy, inequality, and the many stories behind my journey
